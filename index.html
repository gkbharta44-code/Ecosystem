Ecosystem Utility

DPR Token — Medium of Exchange & Tokenomics

The DPR Token is the native medium of exchange within the Delper ecosystem — not solely a value-accrual instrument. Residents receive their $247/month benefit (rent, food, and wallet credit components) in DPR Tokens, which circulate within the ecosystem: spent with local vendors and marketplace sellers (Stage 7), redeemed for platform services, or held as savings. Vendors and sellers who accept DPR Tokens settle supply chain fees and re-circulate tokens back through the ecosystem, creating a closed-loop economy alongside the token's role as a store of value.

Value Accrual Mechanism

Token price is derived directly from Terminal Enterprise Value: Price = EV ÷ 1.98T tokens, with no additional premiums layered on top. As platform net profit grows (Stages 6, 7, and 9), Terminal EV grows at 15× net profit, and token price grows proportionally — the same mechanism used throughout this programme's financial model, with no separate token-specific valuation formula.

Two caveats worth stating plainly. First, a 1.98 trillion token supply is unusually large by real-world standards — most established tokens (Bitcoin: 21M, Ethereum: ~120M) use supplies many orders of magnitude smaller; the large supply here is a direct consequence of dividing an already-unverified Terminal EV by an arbitrary token count, not an independently-designed tokenomics decision. Second, an earlier version of this token price derivation used a broken "premium-stacking" formula (separate from the simple EV÷supply calculation) that produced a disconnected EV progression and a formatting bug displaying percentages instead of dollar prices — this has since been corrected to the simple methodology stated above, and that correction should be verified independently as part of Stage 4's PwC due diligence.

Risk Assessment

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Real, verified DSCR clearance at project level (2.01×) once 13% yield is applied
  • Diversified revenue streams (digital, supply chain, rental, retrofit, employment)
  • Concurrent Phase 1/Phase 2 structure allows small-scale proof before full commitment
  • Retrofit MTN economics independently strong (199,935× project DSCR)

Weaknesses

  • Digital and supply chain terminal revenue figures unverified against real-world markets
  • 13% smart city rental yield exceeds real-world commercial real estate benchmarks
  • City Timeline documentation incomplete (456 of 2,200 cities named)
  • Employment revenue model implies 4.4B jobs — exceeds global labour force

Opportunities

  • Genuine underserved demand for affordable smart-city housing globally
  • KYC-verified audience access is a real, growing premium ad category
  • Phase 1 pilot model de-risks initial government relationships before Phase 2 scale
  • PwC due diligence (Stage 4) can convert unverified figures into bankable ones

Threats

  • Zero of 195 + full-programme sovereign guarantees currently executed
  • Combined capital raise ($1,350.95T) vastly exceeds global bond market capacity
  • Revenue assumptions failing independent scrutiny could derail institutional financing
  • Real estate yield assumption in tension with "low-risk sovereign-guaranteed" framing used for financing
